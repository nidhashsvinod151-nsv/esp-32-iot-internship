# Link to Day 22 Capstone — Deep Sleep Integration Plan

## Connection to Today's CoAP Study
This CoAP overview directly feeds into the Day 22 capstone, "Live
Polyhouse Node." The planned future CR2032 soil moisture sensor will
combine two techniques studied separately so far:

1. CoAP NON messages (today, Day 17) — for power-efficient, connectionless
   telemetry that does not require an open session.
2. ESP32 deep sleep mode — for powering down almost entirely between
   readings.

## Planned Integration Flow
1. ESP32 wakes from deep sleep on a timer (e.g. every 15-60 minutes).
2. Soil moisture sensor is read.
3. A single CoAP NON message is sent with the reading to the Zelbytes
   CoAP resource endpoint.
4. ESP32 immediately re-enters deep sleep without waiting for any
   acknowledgment, since NON messages do not require one.
5. Cycle repeats, with the device spending the overwhelming majority of
   its time asleep to maximize CR2032 battery life.

## Why Not MQTT or HTTPS Here
Both MQTT and HTTPS rely on maintaining or re-establishing a connection,
which costs meaningful radio-on time and battery drain on every cycle.
CoAP's connectionless NON message avoids this entirely, making it the
correct protocol choice for the Day 22 capstone's battery-powered sensor
tier.
